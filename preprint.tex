	\section{Introduction}
	\begin{itemize}
		\item RNA metabolism is dynamic system, speed of reactions = kinetic rates. Kinetic rates are important:
		\begin{itemize}
			\item For example, \citet{vockRNADecayCafeUniformlyProcessed2025} recently estimated that variability in RNA degradation rate explains at least 25\% of variability in transcript abundances between different human cell lines. 
			\item Transcription elongations speed also plays an important role in RNA metabolism. Changes in Pol II speed can affect several co-transcriptional processes, such as splicing, $\text{m}^{6}$A RNA methylation, and polyadenylation \citep{munizRNAPolymeraseII2021}. Recently, a study of \citet{debesAgeingassociatedChangesTranscriptional2023} showed that organism aging is associated with an increase in Pol II speed, whereas lifespan-prolonging interventions tend to decrease it.
			\item The speed of intron splicing (time needed to splice the intron out of the transcript) is another important aspect of RNA metabolism.
		\end{itemize}
		\item Measuring this rates is challenging, dedicated experiments are needed.
		\item Cite here \citet{furlanDynamicsTranscriptionalPosttranscriptional2021}, it's a very relevant review.
		\item Here, we discuss how total RNA-seq can be used to say something about these rates. Total RNA-seq data are common, easy to produce, and large amount of them is already available. However, due to 'static' nature of total RNA-seq data, our measurements will be bit more limited.
		\end{itemize}
	
	\section{Model of RNA metabolism}
	
	Here I will describe the physical model. The ODE or PDE specification will be in Methods section, here I will just show the solution of steady state, and emphasize that measured quantities always depend only on ratios of kinetic rates $\to$ source of non-identifiability. Say that this is a general property of dynamic models:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Consider an autonomous dynamical system with state vector $\boldsymbol{x}$, and with dynamics
		$$
		\dot{\boldsymbol{x}} = f(\boldsymbol{x})
		$$		
		\item It holds that
		$$	
		f(\boldsymbol{x}) = 0 \Leftrightarrow c \cdot f(\boldsymbol{x}) = 0 \quad  \forall c \in \mathbb{R}_+
		$$
		
		\item Therefore, steady-state data cannot distinguish $f(\boldsymbol{x})$ from $c \cdot f(\boldsymbol{x})$.  
		Consequently, only ratios of rates are identifiable.		
		\item This type of structural non-identifiability is common in RNA metabolism models.
	\end{itemize}
	Then I will say that we can estimate difference of LFCs (e.g. LFC(transcription elongation) - LFC(RNA degradation)), and that I implemented model that does that.
